Geographic heat map

GDP per capita shows output relative to population.

A high GDP total can reflect either very high productivity or a very large population. GDP per capita normalizes output by population, making it a clearer comparison of average economic production across countries.

Insight • GDP per capita

GDP and Population

Countries with large populations, such as China and Mexico, may have high total GDP but lower GDP per capita because national output is spread across many residents. In contrast, smaller, highly developed countries such as Ireland and Finland rank higher in GDP per capita due to stronger economic policies, infrastructure, and productivity.

Research Insight • Living standards

Wealth Distribution and Prosperity

Wealth distribution also plays a role in economic well-being. In countries such as Nigeria and Thailand, economic activity does not always translate into higher individual prosperity. This data shows that while large populations can generate substantial wealth, GDP per capita often provides a clearer picture of living standards and economic well-being (McCulla et al., 2015).

Research Insight • Currency conversion

Currency Conversion Clarity

Currency conversions help compare the economic scale and stability of other nations relative to a shared currency. A country’s GDP may appear different when converted into another currency, but a higher converted value does not always equal better living conditions (Shapiro et al., 2022).
